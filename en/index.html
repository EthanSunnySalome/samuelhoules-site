About

Samuel Houles is a French entrepreneur based in Dubai. A former branch manager at Crédit Agricole, he became known for creating Bubble Bump, the network that popularised bubble football in France.

Launched in 2014, the concept grew to more than 70 franchises in under two years and led to a French championship. After building and then selling the brand, Samuel Houles now pursues new entrepreneurial ventures from Dubai.

Career

Banking: Crédit Agricole

Samuel Houles began his career as a branch manager at Crédit Agricole, where he built a solid grounding in management and client relations.

Bubble Bump: the entrepreneurial venture

In 2014, he brought the bubble football concept to France and structured it into a franchise network. In under two years it grew past 70 franchises, hosted a French championship in Toulouse in 2015 and expanded, under the Bump brand, into Archery Bump and Laser Bump. The brand was sold after several years of growth.

Today

Samuel Houles continues to build businesses from Dubai, notably around personal security with the Kronobi project.
